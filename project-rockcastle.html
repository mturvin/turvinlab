Rock Castle Visitor & Event Center

Hendersonville, Tennessee — Tuck-Hinton Architecture & Design

Situated on the historic Rock Castle estate overlooking Old Hickory Lake, the new Visitor and Event Center represents the most significant investment in the site's 240-year history and the largest capital project ever undertaken within Tennessee's State Historic Sites program. The project expands the campus with a new civic and cultural hub that supports public gatherings, educational events, and private functions while honoring the heritage of one of Tennessee's earliest landmarks.

As lead designer and project architect, I guided the project from schematic design through design development, establishing its conceptual and formal direction. Working closely with the client, the Tennessee Historical Commission, and the Office of the State Architect, I helped define a solution that balanced the site's historical significance with the practical demands of a contemporary event facility.

The project included the design of the new Lakeview Hall, extensive site improvements, new parking facilities, and renovations to the caretaker's cottage. I led consultant coordination, managed the BIM model and design documentation, and conducted QA/QC review to ensure technical clarity and constructability. The building now under construction closely reflects the design vision established during my tenure.

Project Details

  • Client: Tennessee Historical Commission
  • Location: Rock Castle State Historic Site, Hendersonville, TN
  • Role: Lead Designer / Project Architect (SD–DD)
  • Firm: Tuck-Hinton Architecture & Design
  • Status: Under Construction
  • Scope: New event center, site improvements, parking, cottage renovation
